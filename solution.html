Solutions to the DDT Problem

Existing Solution

Build a payload attachment to the AUV (Autonomous Underwater Vehicle) and drive it to the contaminated area to deliver the bacteria.

  • Advantages: High accuracy of hitting the target, uses existing technology.
  • Disadvantages: Expensive to operate, limited range up to 6 miles.

Our Solution

Build a robot called *Arihant* that can swim to the contaminated area by leveraging ocean currents and deploy the bacteria effectively.

  • Advantages: Low cost, simpler to deploy, higher coverage area.
  • Disadvantages: Low accuracy (can be mitigated with higher number of robots). Most robots are non-retrievable, potentially littering the ocean floor (can be mitigated by using biodegradable materials).
