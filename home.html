Discover the Problem

What is DDT?

DDT (Dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane) is an effective insecticide, that is colorless, tasteless, and almost odorless.

DDT is highly toxic for humans and marine life, along with insects,causing neurological problems and cancer.

About 40,000 lbs of DDT were dumped in the ocean waters right here near southern California after being banned for their harmful properties!

Dumped DDT barrels are falling apart due to natural corrosion and are contaminating surrounding areas, influencing marine ecosystems.

Researchers from Scripps Institution of Oceanography identified high levels of contamination of DDT in the areas.

Why Should You Care?

    DDT accumulates in the food chain, harming top predators and humans.

    Contaminated water affects aquatic life and fishing industries.

    DDT's toxicity poses risks to reproductive health and development.

    DDT was also linked to health issues such as cancer, reproductive problems, and neurological effects in humans.
